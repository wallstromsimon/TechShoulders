Carnegie Mellon University is a private research university in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania. Its School of Computer Science is consistently ranked among the best in the world, and the university has been central to advances in artificial intelligence, robotics, and software engineering.

## Computing Pioneers

CMU has been home to many computing pioneers:

- Allen Newell and Herbert Simon founded AI research here
- The university pioneered computer networking with ARPANET
- The Software Engineering Institute is headquartered at CMU
- The Robotics Institute is a world leader in autonomous systems

## Key Contributions

CMU research has produced influential technologies:

- Early work on AI and cognitive science
- Development of speech recognition systems
- Pioneering research in computer vision
- Self-driving vehicle technology

## Influence on Industry

Many technology leaders trace their education or research to CMU. The university's emphasis on practical computer science education and its close ties to industry have produced generations of engineers and entrepreneurs.
