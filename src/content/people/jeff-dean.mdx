Jeff Dean (born 1968) is an American computer scientist and Google Senior Fellow known for designing Google's most critical infrastructure systems. His work with Sanjay Ghemawat created the foundations of modern large-scale computing.

## Google Infrastructure

Dean co-designed numerous foundational systems:

- **MapReduce** (2004): Parallel data processing model
- **BigTable** (2006): Distributed storage for structured data
- **Spanner** (2012): Globally distributed database
- **TensorFlow** (2015): Machine learning framework

## The Jeff Dean Facts

Dean became a legend in tech, inspiring "Jeff Dean Facts"—humorous exaggerations of his coding abilities (similar to Chuck Norris facts). They reflect genuine respect for his extraordinary productivity and system design skills.

## Technical Contributions

Beyond named systems, Dean contributed to:

- Google Search ranking improvements
- Ad serving infrastructure
- Protocol Buffers (with Sanjay Ghemawat)
- Compiler optimizations

## Leadership

Dean leads Google's AI Research division, overseeing:

- Google Brain team
- DeepMind collaboration
- Large language model development
- AI infrastructure and ethics

## Recognition

Dean has received numerous awards including:

- National Academy of Engineering membership
- ACM Prize in Computing (2012)
- ACM-Infosys Foundation Award

His career exemplifies how individual engineers can shape the technical direction of trillion-dollar companies.
